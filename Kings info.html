Hatshepsut

1507 - 1458 BC

Hatshepsut was named the most successful female pharaoh in ascending the throne of Egypt. Hatshepsut , the fifth ruler of the Eighteenth Dynasty in Egypt.

Thutmose III

1481 - 1425 BC

The eighteenth king of Egypt and stepson of the former pharaoh Hatshepsut. He was originally supposed to take the throne after the death of Thutmose II, but he was not of legal age

Ramses II

1303 – 1213 BC

He is considered among the pharaohs of Egypt, the third pharaoh of the nineteenth dynasty, and one of the greatest pharaohs in the New Kingdom of Egypt. Egyptians often call him “the great-grandfather.”

Amenhotep III

1391 - 1353 BC

The ninth pharaoh of the eighteenth dynasty had a great influence on the Egyptian economy, which flourished during his reign thanks to the healthy trade relations at this time.

Tutankhamun

1341 - 1323 BC

The most famous pharaoh of all is King Tutankhamun. He ascended the throne at the age of nine or ten. His fame comes from the discovery of his almost intact tomb

Akhenaten

1380 - 1334 BC

An ancient pharaoh of the Eighteenth Dynasty. He advocated monotheism, or the worship of only one God. This god was Aton, a type of solar deity associated with the god Ra
